Apple A round fruit with red or green skin and a crisp or juicy flesh.
Tiger A large carnivorous feline mammal with a yellowish-brown coat striped with black.
Music The art or science of combining vocal or instrumental sounds to produce beauty of form, harmony, and expression of emotion.
Ocean A very large expanse of sea, in particular, each of the main areas into which the sea is divided geographically.
Dream A series of thoughts, images, and sensations occurring in a person's mind during sleep.
Pizza A dish of Italian origin consisting of a flat, round base of dough baked with a topping of tomato sauce and cheese, typically with added meat or vegetables.
River A large natural stream of water flowing in a channel to the sea, a lake, or another such stream.
Sun A star around which the earth orbits.
Moon The natural satellite of the earth, visible (chiefly at night) by reflected light from the sun.
Book A written or printed work consisting of pages glued or sewn together along one side and bound in covers.
Car A road vehicle, typically with four wheels, powered by an internal combustion engine and able to carry a small number of people.
Train A series of connected railroad cars pulled or pushed by one or more locomotives.
Chair A separate seat for one person, typically with a back and four legs.
Phone A telephone.
Clock A mechanical or electrical device for measuring time, typically in hours and minutes, and striking or making a sound at specific intervals.
